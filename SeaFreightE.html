Sea Shipping

Modern sea shipping has had a significant impact on the world of freight transport. Currently, most commercial transactions between various continents are carried out via sea shipping. As Iraq is a key commercial hub in the Arabian Gulf and the Middle East, Bridges of the Seas offers the best solutions for shipping goods and various materials by sea through Iraq’s different ports, providing maximum flexibility in transport and shipping while maintaining a limited cost range that suits customers' needs, whether for full containers, less-than-container-load (LCL), or bulk sea shipping.

Bridges of the Seas is a specialized company in sea shipping operations to and from most ports using global shipping line vessels, whether full container loads (FCL) or less-than-container-load (LCL). The company handles all port and customs procedures to facilitate shipping for its clients.

Sea Shipping Systems

  • Full Container Shipping (FCL): Suitable for clients wishing to ship large quantities, with all of the client’s goods shipped in a dedicated full container.
  • Partial Shipping (LCL): Goods are shipped within part of a container along with other shipments to reduce costs, though some expenses may be higher.

Types of Sea Containers

  • 20-Foot Container: Capacity 30 m³, weight 18 tons.
  • 40-Foot Container: Capacity 60 m³, weight 22 tons.
  • 40-Foot HQ Container: Capacity 68 m³, weight 22 tons.
  • Open Top Container (OT): Open from the top, for high loads.
  • Refrigerated Containers (20 & 40 Feet): For transporting perishable goods at controlled temperatures.
  • Special Containers: Such as flat-rack or platform containers, for equipment and irregular loads.
