Steps for Air Freight Shipment with Jessor Al Behr

Order Submission:

The process starts with submitting an air freight request by the client through direct contact with customer service, including shipment details such as weight, dimensions, type, shipping address, delivery address, and expected shipping date.

Shipment Evaluation:

The shipment is evaluated by the Jessor Al Behr logistics team to determine the best handling method and identify any special requirements, such as packaging and necessary permits.

Packaging:

Jessor Al Behr prepares the shipment for air freight by proper packaging, including using appropriate packing materials and securing the shipment to ensure its safety during transport.

Document Preparation:

This step includes preparing the required shipping documents such as the shipping invoice, contents list, customs clearance declaration, and the Airway Bill, along with any other documents required based on the sending and receiving countries and the type of goods.

Customs Clearance:

Air freight typically involves customs clearance, where Jessor Al Behr coordinates with customs authorities to ensure compliance with customs requirements and facilitate the shipment's passage through airports.

Transportation and Delivery:

After preparing the shipment and completing the necessary procedures, the shipment is loaded onto the aircraft and transported to its destination. Upon arrival, the shipment is unloaded and delivered to the final recipient at the airport or to the recipient’s doorstep, as per the client's request.

Shipment Tracking:

Jessor Al Behr offers a shipment tracking service where the client can monitor the shipment’s status and location during the air transport process.

Essential Documents for Air Freight Shipment

When shipping via air freight, the client must have certain essential documents. The requirements may vary depending on the countries involved and the type of goods. Some of the most important documents include:

  • Shipping Invoice: A document containing shipment details such as the goods being sent, the quantity, value, and pricing.
  • Contents List: Specifies the materials and goods in the shipment, including weight, dimensions, and quantities.
  • Airway Bill: The primary document that formalizes the contract between the shipping company and the client, containing shipment details.
  • Customs Documents: Such as customs declaration, certificate of origin, and necessary export/import permits.
  • Insurance and Guarantee Documents: Such as an insurance certificate or shipment guarantee document.
  • Documents for Prohibited or Restricted Goods: Special permits for shipping hazardous or biological materials.
